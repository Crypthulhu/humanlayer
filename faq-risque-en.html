Risk & Accountability

What happens when things don't go as planned

Decision error

As with any professional decision:

  • responsibility lies with the identified human who made the decision
  • within their qualifications, authorizations, and legal obligations

HumanLayer:

  • never decides
  • never modifies the decision
  • never hides the decision maker's identity

ðŸ‘‰ HumanLayer provides the evidence, not the decision.

Abuse protection

HumanLayer implements several safeguards:

  • strict scope of authorized decisions
  • full logging of all actions
  • anomaly detection (atypical decision patterns)
  • possibility of immediate suspension

Client organizations can:

  • exclude a decision maker
  • require a second opinion
  • trigger a targeted audit

ðŸ‘‰ Abuse is more visible, not less.

AI context

HumanLayer:

  • logs exactly the context provided by the AI
  • timestamps the information
  • keeps versions

The human decision maker:

  • decides based on that context
  • can refuse or request more information

ðŸ‘‰ In case of incident, it's always possible to determine:

  • what the AI provided
  • what the human saw
  • what the decision was based on

Prohibited uses

No.

HumanLayer explicitly rejects:

  • illegal decisions
  • uses intended to circumvent regulations
  • requests outside the defined scope

Automatic rejection rules can be configured.
Certain categories of decisions are excluded by design.

Responsibility allocation

  • The decision: the human decision maker
  • The usage framework: the client organization
  • Orchestration and traceability: HumanLayer

This clear separation is essential and contractual.

HumanLayer is not a decision-making actor.

Its liability is limited to:

  • proper service execution
  • traceability
  • availability per SLA

ðŸ‘‰ It is an infrastructure provider, not a decision maker.

Insurance and protection

Depending on the decision level:

  • decision makers operate within their usual professional framework
  • some decisions may require specific insurance
  • additional coverage can be offered as an enterprise option

The exact terms are contractual and transparent.

Real-time blocking

Yes.

Organizations can define:

  • risk thresholds
  • automatic rejection rules
  • mandatory escalations
  • multi-signature validations

ðŸ‘‰ A decision cannot slip through unnoticed.

In summary:
HumanLayer doesn't eliminate risk.
It makes it visible, traceable, and attributable.
